## Open questions a partner would ask

1. **Identity reconciliation:** Is the LedgerLoop being pitched the same legal entity as the Cape Town accounting practice? If yes, what's the pivot from "South African SME accounting" to "embedded working-capital and cashflow tools for Southeast Asian wholesalers"? If no, please share the company website, deck, or registration of the entity actually being evaluated — public search returned zero hits for a SE Asia LedgerLoop.
2. **Domain fit for SEA:** Why Southeast Asia? The founder's only documented presence is South Africa; no SEA tour-of-duty, language fit, channel relationships, or local advisor disclosed. Has he co-founded with someone in-region? Who?
3. **Bull & Bear Markets due diligence:** What was the outcome — is it still operating, did it wind down, were there any token-holder disputes? Founder-claimed traction ($3M beta volume, 6k users) needs independent verification (on-chain analytics, exchange data) before counting as a fintech-product win.
4. **Concurrent commitments:** He is currently full-time at *both* LucroTech (CEO, Aug 2025 →) and LedgerLoop Accountants (founder, Jun 2025 →). Adding a SEA fintech as the primary deal would be his third concurrent full-time role. What's the time allocation, and is he willing to step away from one?
5. **Working-capital underwriting credibility:** His credibility is accounting + crypto/DeFi product. Embedded working capital requires credit underwriting, capital-markets relationships, and licensing experience. Who on the team brings that — and have they shipped a credit product before?
6. **Why "LedgerLoop" twice?** Reusing the brand of a still-operating accounting practice for an unrelated fintech product is unusual; clarify the IP / brand intent.
