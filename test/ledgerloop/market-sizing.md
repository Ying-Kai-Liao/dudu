# Market sizing: LedgerLoop

**Deal:** ledgerloop
**Generated:** 2026-04-28T09:20:00Z
**Method:** Bottom-up from public SA SME population data, with no anchoring on any founder-supplied TAM (none was disclosed publicly anyway).

> **Reframe note:** The original deal pitch ("embedded working-capital and cashflow tools for Southeast Asian wholesalers") would have produced an entirely different TAM. The reframed product is a Cape Town-based modern accounting/tax practice for SA SMEs and freelancers — and that's what is sized below.

> **Critical regulatory update relevant to sizing:** South Africa raised the compulsory VAT-registration threshold from **R1m to R2.3m** with effect from **1 April 2026** ([Business Tech Africa](https://www.businesstechafrica.co.za/business/2026/04/14/vat-threshold-increase-a-practical-win-for-south-african-smes/), [Lula](https://lula.co.za/blog/sme-advice/vat-threshold-south-africa/)). This **materially weakens the freelancer-VAT-trigger thesis** that surfaced as Persona 2's hot button — a freelancer at R900k–R1.5m revenue is no longer near the trigger. The wedge sizing below uses the post-April-2026 thresholds.

## Wedge ICP

> A Cape Town-based, formally registered Pty (Ltd) or VAT-registered sole-proprietor SME with **R2m–R20m annual turnover**, owner-managed, currently using Pastel desktop or Xero for in-house bookkeeping plus an external CA at year-end — triggered to switch by a SARS query/dispute, VAT registration crossing, or external-CA dissatisfaction — willing to pay R4,500–R17,500/month for consolidated outsourced accounting + tax + payroll.

This is narrower than LedgerLoop's marketed ICP of "freelancers, SMEs, and startups." Two reasons for narrowing:
1. The **R4,500/mo entry tier prices out most freelancers** (Persona 2's modeled WTP ceiling is ~R3,000/mo — see [round-3.md](personas/round-3.md), [round-4.md](personas/round-4.md)). A retainer model below R4,500/mo is not LedgerLoop's published product.
2. The **R17,500/mo top tier prices out micro-businesses** but fits services SMEs in the R5–20m turnover band (Persona 3, [round-5.md](personas/round-5.md), [round-6.md](personas/round-6.md)).

The midpoint persona — a startup founder doing R2–10m turnover — is what the R9,500/mo "Growing" tier targets. **This is the load-bearing segment for LedgerLoop's economics.**

## Wedge TAM math

```
Cape Town-only, R2m–R20m owner-managed VAT-vendor cohort (static SAM):
~12,000 SMEs static reachable × R100k blended ACV = R1.2 billion / year (~US$65m)

Annual addressable shopper subset (the realistic year-on-year wedge SAM):
Low:  1,200 × R54,000  =  R64.8 million / year (~US$3.5m)
Mid:  2,500 × R100,000 =  R250 million / year (~US$13.5m)
High: 3,800 × R210,000 =  R798 million / year (~US$43m)
```

**Wedge TAM (Cape Town, annual cohort of in-market shoppers):** **R65m–R800m / yr (~$3.5m–$43m)**.
**Static CPT SAM (full owner-managed VAT-vendor pool in band):** **~R1.2bn / yr (~$65m)**.

These are *market-sized* numbers, not LedgerLoop's capturable share. With ~17 named direct competitors in [competitive-landscape.md](competitive-landscape.md) and an undifferentiated brand position, a realistic 5-year capture for a well-executed CPT-focused practice is roughly **0.5–2% of the static SAM** = R6m–R24m ARR (~$0.3–$1.3m). That is a small services business, not a venture-scale outcome.

## Expansion segments

### Segment 1 — Pan-SA expansion (beyond Cape Town)

- **Reachable:** ~497k active VAT vendors nationwide × ~50–60% in R2m–R20m × ~70% owner-managed × ~10–15% in-market = ~17,000–31,000 annual shoppers (SA-wide).
- **ACV:** R54k–R210k (same).
- **TAM (annual):** **R900m–R6.5bn / yr (~$50m–$350m)**.
- **Static SAM (full SA owner-managed R2–20m VAT cohort):** ~170k–210k × R100k = **~R17–21bn (~$900m–$1.1bn)**. This roughly matches the [Grand View Research SA outsourced accounting projection of US$3.15bn by 2030](https://www.grandviewresearch.com/industry-analysis/south-africa-business-process-outsourcing-market) when broader BPO scope is factored in.
- **Risk:** services scale linearly with people, not capital. Multi-city expansion is operationally hard and quality-dependent. Most named competitors ([competitive-landscape.md](competitive-landscape.md)) are JHB / WC concentrated and have not successfully scaled nationally past 200–500 clients each.

### Segment 2 — Vertical specialization (crypto-active SMEs / SaaS-founder cohort)

- **Reachable — SaaS founders:** [Tracxn lists 1,902 SA SaaS startups](https://tracxn.com/d/explore/saas-startups-in-south-africa/__Z2nINtPwbVoAJrpA24xLRaFPLyx72RUaHfzHilA8YGo/companies). The fundable / paying subset is plausibly 5–10% = 95–190.
- **Reachable — crypto-active SA SMEs:** No published count; SARS treats crypto disposals as taxable but the formal-business overlap is small. Plausible ~500–2,000 SA crypto-active companies.
- **ACV:** Premium $200–$500/mo (R3,700–R9,250/mo) common for cross-border SaaS accounting per Inkle pricing comparables; potentially higher for dual-entity SaaS founders.
- **TAM (annual):** **R30m–R200m / yr (~$1.5–$11m)**. Too small to be a wedge on its own; viable as a high-margin niche layer atop a generalist practice.
- **Strategic value:** vertical specialization is a *positioning* asset more than a sizing asset — it gives LedgerLoop a defensible "we do crypto / dual-entity SaaS" claim that no incumbent matches.

### Segment 3 — Productize a SARS-localized SaaS layer (Inkle-equivalent for SA)

- **Reachable:** Self-serve software has dramatically larger reachable population — closer to the full ~497k active VAT vendors plus all sole-prop micro-businesses = ~600k–700k SA businesses willing to spend on accounting software.
- **ACV:** R6,000–R24,000/yr per user (R500–R2,000/mo, comparable to Xero ZA + add-ons or Inkle's $200–$400/mo).
- **TAM (annual, SaaS economics):** **R3.6bn–R17bn / yr (~$190m–$900m)** total category, of which a successful new entrant might capture 0.5–2% = $1m–$18m ARR over 5 years.
- **Why it matters:** this is the only segment with venture-compatible economics. **It is also a different company** from a services practice — it requires a software team, distribution, capital, and 3–5 years of product investment that LedgerLoop has not signaled.

### Segment 4 — SADC region (Namibia, Botswana, Zimbabwe, Mozambique, Lesotho)

- **Reachable:** Combined SADC-ex-SA SME population is approximately 30% of SA's, ~150k–200k formal SMEs.
- **ACV:** Lower than SA (~R30k–R100k/yr) due to local price points.
- **TAM (annual):** **R5bn–R20bn (~$270m–$1.1bn)** combined static SAM.
- **Risk:** each new country requires re-localization for a different tax regime, currency, and compliance system. Capital-intensive; not realistic for a 4-month-old practice in the next 2 years.

## Verdict on wedge

- **Clearly defined?** **Partial.** LedgerLoop's marketed ICP ("freelancers, SMEs, startups") is too broad to be a wedge. The implicit wedge that fits the published price tiers — Cape Town owner-managed Pty (Ltd) / VAT-vendor SMEs in the R2–R20m turnover band — is defensible, but the founder hasn't articulated it that way publicly. **Sharpening the ICP would be the most valuable action item from this exercise.**
- **Reachable?** **Yes — but only at high CAC.** ~1,200–3,800 in-market shoppers in CPT in any given year are *findable* (LinkedIn, Silicon Cape, Heavy Chef, Vistage SA, peer referrals — see [customer-discovery-prep.md](customer-discovery-prep.md)). They are **not findable through paid SEO/SEM** at competitive economics: the personas were uniformly clear that they ignore generic "modern accountant" marketing copy. CAC will be dominated by referral-network density and content-credibility build, not paid acquisition. This is a slow-burn flywheel, not a venture-scale acquisition curve.
- **Credible expansion path?** **Partial.** Pan-SA expansion is real but capital-intensive and operationally fragile (services scale poorly). The vertical-specialization wedge (crypto / dual-entity SaaS) gives positioning but not enough TAM to matter. **The only segment with venture-compatible economics is SaaS productization — and that is a different company that LedgerLoop has not signaled it will become.** SADC expansion is a 5+ year story.

## Open questions

1. **What is LedgerLoop's actual capturable share assumption?** This bottom-up sizing puts the realistic 5-year ARR ceiling at ~R6–24m for a CPT-focused practice (~$0.3–$1.3m). If the founder's plan is bigger, the difference is in expansion (pan-SA / SaaS / SADC) — and each expansion vector has different capital and team requirements that should be explicit.
2. **What is the SAICA-side competitive intensity?** SAICA has ~52,000 CA(SA) members ([Wikipedia / SAICA profile](https://www.saica.org.za/Technical/Ethics/Howtolodgeacomplaint/tabid/3139/language/en-ZA/Default.aspx)); a non-trivial slice of those run their own SME-facing practices. The supply side is not capital-constrained.
3. **How does the April 2026 VAT threshold change to R2.3m affect LedgerLoop's freelancer-trigger marketing?** If the freelancer wedge was leaning on the old R1m threshold, this is a material strategic update and the website / sales narrative should be re-checked.
4. **What is the actual customer-discovery-tested capture rate?** The ratios above (10–15% in-market in any given year, 0.5–2% capturable share) are conservative B2B-services-norm assumptions. Real inputs from the VC's interviews could move both numbers materially.
5. **Is there published data on SA SME accountant churn / switching rate?** This was a "we couldn't find" item in [_context.md](personas/_context.md) and remains the single biggest unknown — it sets the in-market shopper share each year.
6. **Has LedgerLoop priced productization explicitly off the table, or is that the long-term plan?** The biggest TAM gap is between services economics ($55m–$400m) and SaaS economics ($190m–$900m). The deal's investability hinges on which of these LedgerLoop is positioning toward.
