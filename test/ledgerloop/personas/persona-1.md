# Persona 1: Early-stage SaaS founder

**Role:** Co-founder & CEO of a 3-person Cape Town SaaS startup; ex-engineer; first-time founder
**Demographics:** 31, based in Sea Point, no in-house finance staff, married, 1 kid. Pre-seed (~R3m raised from a SA angel and a US accelerator). Revenue mostly USD (Stripe to a US Inc parent), some ZAR contracts. ~R200k MRR equivalent run-rate, growing 8-10% MoM.
**Current workflow:** Bank feeds into Xero, manual receipt entry by the COO partner two evenings a month, year-end FS done by an outside CA at R12k flat. SARS eFiling self-managed; missed first provisional last year and got hit with a penalty. Uses a US accountant at the parent level for the Delaware C-corp; the SA Pty (Pty) Ltd is the operating entity.
**Pain intensity (1–10):** 7 — not a daily fire, but a recurring three-day distraction every quarter and a real risk surface (missed VAT threshold, ESOP accounting unknown, dual-entity transfer pricing opaque). Penalty letters cause acute anxiety. Has lost two evenings to "what tier of provisional tax am I in?" googling.
**Willingness-to-pay anchor:** R6,000–R10,000/month is the band he tolerates because that's roughly what he was already paying his year-end CA + Xero + receipt tool combined, annualized. Anchored by Inkle's pricing for US-Indian YC-style cohorts (~$200–$400/mo) which is where his benchmarking comes from. Above R12k/mo he starts asking "should I just hire a part-timer?" Source: context bundle entry-tier pricing band (R1,990–R4,500) and his own prior CA spend.
**Voice / phrasing:** Engineer-blunt. "I just want it handled." "Show me the API." "If I can solve it once and never think about it again, I'll pay 2x." Terms-of-art: "ESOP," "transfer pricing," "burn," "runway," "MRR." Skeptical of AI marketing copy ("everyone says AI now"); responsive to demonstrable workflow ("show me a sample close").
