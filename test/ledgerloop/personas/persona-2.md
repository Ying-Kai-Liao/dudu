# Persona 2: Freelance designer crossing the VAT threshold

**Role:** Senior freelance brand & UX designer, sole proprietor (no Pty Ltd), 8 years self-employed
**Demographics:** 38, based in Johannesburg (Linden), works mostly with mid-size SA agencies and 2 European studios paying in EUR. ~R900k expected 2026 revenue (R75k/month average), trending up ~15% YoY. Single, no kids, owns a flat. Has been a provisional taxpayer for 6 years.
**Current workflow:** Excel spreadsheet for invoices, separate sheet for expenses tracked from FNB business bank statements. SARS eFiling self-managed, twice yearly provisional tax based on prior-year proxy. Receipts kept in a Google Drive folder, sometimes not. Year-end ITR12 he files himself (proudly), but the last two years he paid R3,500 to a small accountancy firm (a one-CA shop in Randburg) to "check it over" before submission. Has never been audited. Got a SARS auto-assessment last year that overstated income — took 5 weeks of back-and-forth on eFiling to correct. Crossed R1m once in 2023 invoiced (paid in 2024) and never registered for VAT — knows this is a risk.
**Pain intensity (1–10):** 6 — chronic low-grade anxiety rather than a sharp pain. Thinks about it on Sunday evenings. Spikes to 9 when he gets the SARS auto-assessment letter. Knows enough to know what he doesn't know, especially around the VAT threshold and forex-paid invoices.
**Willingness-to-pay anchor:** R1,500–R3,000/month maximum, justified by the R3,500 annual fee he already pays for ITR12 review (R290/month equivalent) plus a willingness to roughly 5x that for "peace of mind." Source: context bundle entry-tier band starting at R1,990 — that's literally the price he benchmarks against and finds the upper bound of palatable. R4,500/month (LedgerLoop's "Starting Out" tier) is *above* his ceiling and he'd self-rationalise as overpriced for his volume.
**Voice / phrasing:** Calm, slightly self-deprecating. "Look, I'm a designer not an accountant — I just don't want SARS sending me a letter." Uses words like "freaking out," "cooked," "the dreaded SARS letter." Doesn't use accounting terms; will say "VAT thing" or "tax estimate thing." Trusts personal-recommendation referrals more than websites.
