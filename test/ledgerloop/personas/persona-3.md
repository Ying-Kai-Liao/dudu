# Persona 3: Growing services SME, vendor-fatigued

**Role:** Founder & MD of a 12-person Durban-based digital marketing agency, 7 years operating
**Demographics:** 44, married, 2 teenage kids. Pty Ltd, VAT-registered, ~R8m FY2025 turnover, ~R1.4m EBITDA. PAYE-registered for 11 staff (1 contractor). 3 of the 12 are part-time/UIF-exempt. Banks with Investec; uses Sage Pastel Partner desktop (yes, still desktop) plus a separate SimplePay payroll subscription.
**Current workflow:** In-house junior bookkeeper (R22k/mo gross) handles day-to-day Pastel data entry and SimplePay runs. Annual financial statements + tax done by a 4-partner CA firm in Umhlanga (~R45k–R60k/year all-in, billed in tranches). VAT201 prepared by junior, reviewed by external CA before submission. PAYE/EMP201 done by junior. Year-end is messy — bookkeeper exports trial balance from Pastel, CA firm rebuilds in their own software, MD chases revisions. CA firm rotates the manager-on-account roughly yearly, which has been a pain point. Has switched CA firm twice in 7 years.
**Pain intensity (1–10):** 8 — it's not the work itself, it's the **vendor sprawl + accountability fog**. When something goes wrong (e.g. a SARS query on VAT input on an EUR-denominated invoice), nobody owns it. The CA blames the bookkeeper, the bookkeeper says "I just enter what's there." MD spends a real ~6 hours/month adjudicating. Acute spike at year-end (Feb–April closing).
**Willingness-to-pay anchor:** R15,000–R22,000/month, justified by the all-in current spend: R22k bookkeeper + ~R5k/mo amortised CA fee + ~R600/mo SimplePay = R27k/mo. Would consolidate to ~R20k/mo if she could fire the in-house bookkeeper and have one accountable provider. LedgerLoop's R17,500 "Outsourcing" tier is exactly the band she'd evaluate. Source: context bundle top-tier R17,500/mo and competitor comparable spend; her existing combined cost structure.
**Voice / phrasing:** Direct, time-poor. "I don't care which person does it, I care that someone's name is on it." "I've been burned by 'modern' agencies before." Asks for references. Uses business terms fluently — VAT201, EMP201, EBITDA, IT14, IRP5 — but is not herself a finance person. Trusts references from her peer-CEO network in Durban / KZN business associations.
