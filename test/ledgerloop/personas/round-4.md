# Round 4 — Persona 2 (Joburg freelance designer)

**Generated:** 2026-04-28T08:46:00Z
**Mom-Test focus:** word of mouth, search behaviour, decision criteria when actually shopping.

## Conversation

**Interviewer:** Imagine the moment six months from now where you finally decide to get a proper accountant. Walk me through how you'd find one.

**Persona 2:** First place I'd ask: a WhatsApp group of about 14 freelance designers I'm in. "Anyone using a good accountant?" Then I'd Google "accountant for freelancers Joburg." I'd probably get like five websites that look the same — stock photos of smiling people, the words "trusted partner" and "tech-driven." I'd open three tabs, look at pricing, close two of them, then probably do nothing for two weeks.

**Interviewer:** What would make you actually book a call?

**Persona 2:** A specific person's name in a recommendation. "Use Thandi at X, she handled my VAT registration end-to-end." Or — a price that's listed on the website without a "contact us." If I have to fill out a form to get a price I assume it's expensive and I close the tab.

**Interviewer:** When you've looked at modern accounting firm websites, what's stuck?

**Persona 2:** They all sound like LinkedIn AI summaries of each other. "Empower your business." "Focus on what matters." It's noise. The ones that stuck were the ones with actual case studies — "We helped this client save R40k in penalties" — but those are rare. Most are pure marketing.

**Interviewer:** Have you ever paid for any accounting tool, like Xero or QuickBooks?

**Persona 2:** No. I tried Xero's free trial, found it overwhelming, went back to the spreadsheet. The honest truth is my spreadsheet is fine. The bookkeeping isn't the hard part — it's the SARS interface and the regulatory anxiety. Software doesn't fix that.

**Interviewer:** Interesting — so the thing you'd pay for isn't bookkeeping, it's...

**Persona 2:** It's a human who will look at my situation, tell me what I'm actually supposed to be doing, file the right things, and absorb the SARS stress for me. The "modern" part — automation, AI — I don't care if it's running on a Commodore 64 as long as someone responsive is on the other end.

**Interviewer:** When the family-friend CA referred you — what closed it?

**Persona 2:** Honestly? It was free. He said "just send me the form, R2k done." I had no other quote. There was no decision. I just used what was in front of me. I think that's how most freelancers end up where they end up.

**Interviewer:** And when you'd switch — what would the trigger be?

**Persona 2:** The VAT threshold. And maybe — if I get audited. I haven't, but if I did, I think I'd panic-google on a Saturday morning and call whoever picks up. Pricing would matter less than availability in that moment.

**Interviewer:** If a service charged you R2,500/mo flat including VAT registration and the bi-monthly returns, would you sign up?

**Persona 2:** [Long pause] Maybe. I'd want to talk to one customer first who looks like me. Not a startup founder, not a Pty Ltd — a freelance designer or copywriter at my revenue level. If their experience was good, yes. Without that reference, no.
