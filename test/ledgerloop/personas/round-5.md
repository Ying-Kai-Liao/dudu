## Conversation

**Interviewer:** You mentioned switching CA firms twice in 7 years. Tell me about the most recent switch.

**Persona 3:** Two years ago. We were with a 20-partner firm in Westville, paying about R55k a year for full year-end plus tax. The trigger was a SARS query on input VAT we'd claimed on a EUR-denominated supplier invoice — it sat in their queue for six weeks while the manager-on-account changed twice. By the time someone got back to me, the dispute window was almost closed. We lost R18k of input VAT we should have gotten. I gave 30 days notice the next morning.

**Interviewer:** How did you find the new firm?

**Persona 3:** A peer in our KZN CEO peer group recommended them. I had three names, I called all three, two had week-out availability for a meeting, one took the meeting in two days. That speed was the tiebreaker.

**Interviewer:** What were you specifically asking in those meetings?

**Persona 3:** Three things. One: who specifically would be on my account, and would that person change. Two: their response-time SLA on SARS queries, in writing. Three: how they handle multi-currency suppliers and exchange-rate gain/loss treatment, because that's where I'd already been burned. The firm I picked answered all three concretely. The other two gave me marketing-speak.

**Interviewer:** Two years in, are you happy?

**Persona 3:** Mostly. They're better than the previous firm but the in-house bookkeeper / external CA split is still a problem. When something goes sideways, my bookkeeper says "the CA didn't tell me to do it that way" and the CA says "well your bookkeeper entered it wrong." Nobody owns the chain. I'm thinking about firing the bookkeeper and consolidating to one external provider that handles bookkeeping AND tax AND payroll under one bill.

**Interviewer:** Have you priced that?

**Persona 3:** Yes. Three quotes. R18k/mo, R22k/mo, and R28k/mo respectively. The R22k one — a Joburg fixed-fee firm — was promising but they wanted a 6-month onboarding phase to migrate from Pastel desktop to Xero, and the founder couldn't tell me how disruptive that would be. The R28k one was a big-name firm and felt like I'd be a small client again. The R18k one had three clients my size and showed me sample monthly packs. That was compelling but I'm worried about depth on multi-currency.

**Interviewer:** What's stopping you from picking the R18k one?

**Persona 3:** Pastel migration risk. My bookkeeper has 6 years of history in Pastel Partner with custom expense codes I can't even fully reconstruct. If the migration drops fidelity I lose visibility. The migration is the gate, not the price.

**Interviewer:** When you say "modern accounting firm," what does that mean to you?

**Persona 3:** Honestly? Cloud-based, fixed-fee, response within a day, sends me a monthly pack proactively without me chasing. That's it. The "AI" stuff doesn't move me — I assume any 2025 firm is using it. What I want is *operational discipline* and *named accountability*. Show me a partner's name on every email, not "the team."

**Interviewer:** What would it take to add a fourth quote tomorrow?

**Persona 3:** A specific peer recommendation. Or a case study of someone in services, R5–10m turnover, who migrated off Pastel without losing data fidelity. That's the proof I'm looking for. Marketing claims don't move me.
